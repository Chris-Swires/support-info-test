Research Findings

Summary: Myocarditis due to Kawasaki disease is a serious condition that can arise as a complication of Kawasaki disease, an illness primarily affecting children that causes inflammation of blood vessels. Leading to potential heart complications like myocarditis.

Clinical Evidence: Research indicates that myocarditis can occur in children who suffer from Kawasaki disease, presenting significant risks such as heart failure and arrhythmias. Studies have shown a notable incidence rate of myocarditis as a result of Kawasaki disease [2].

Modeling Assessment: The SNOMED CT model correctly defines myocarditis due to Kawasaki disease under the broader category of diseases of the cardiovascular system, accurately reflecting the pathology of myocarditis in this context.

Recommendations: It is recommended to enhance the definition of the condition with specific clinical criteria for diagnosis and management strategies reflecting current guidelines.
